March 24, 2007 - Plane Crash Site

A Martin PBM Mariner aircraft. These were used for reconnaissance and anti-submarine actions.

click for pictures

 

On November 30, 1944 a military PBM aircraft, also known as a flying boat, crashed into the  south  side of Mt. Tamalpais. The pilot and all eight passengers died in the crash. The following excerpts are from the crash report.

 

...experienced engine trouble when about over the Golden Gate Bridge, made a wide turn to the right, and in the resultant confusion naturally did not keep a sharp, continuous lookout; forward vision also being hindered by the darkness and rain conditions.

Just prior to the accident the aircraft flew over a ridge one-half mile from the scene of the crash and it is believed knocked down a telephone pole and broke electric wires. However, the pilot maintained control of the aircraft as the plane crashed into the mountain in a level altitude and at the same altitude as the electric wires

 

The full crash report is here.

Specifications for the airplane are here.
