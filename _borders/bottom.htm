February 18, 2006 - Snow on Tam

Bob on Rock Springs Lagunitas Fire Road at Potrero Meadow.

Friday afternoon a dusting of snow  turned Tamalpais white at the West Point Inn level and above. Footsteps in the East Peak parking lot and around the peak on Verna Dunshee Trail left inch-and-a-half deep impressions. By Saturday morning it was down to patches but we did have a couple snow flurries.

February 11, 2006 - The Throne

Dave at The Throne

The Throne is in an open, sunny view spot in a hobbit landscape. There is a shrine here and people come to be with their thoughts among nature's best. Many scraggly Sargent Cypress trees grow around boulders in fantastic shapes like a bonsai forest made life-size. The throne itself is a large armchair made from piled rocks. In the past it was a single chair but has recently evolved into a two-and-a-half seater. It faces the the setting sun.

February 4, 2006 - Anchor Tree

Jakub thinking maybe he heard something in the swamp... An early sign of mental fatigue? Later, he began behaving a bit, uh - well, check out the photos.

Most of this adventure involved trails not shown on the map. We did some normal stuff like Cataract Trail, Benstein Trail, Swede George Trail, parts of Kent and Stocking trails, but also things with names like Dipsea Boy Shortcut, Fairly Carvin', and Scary-Dude-With-The-Knife Trail. The Anchor Tree itself is an old Douglas Fir growing from the bank of a remote little stream. At some point it must have been  knocked over and sprouted what is now the main trunk from its middle; the original trunk forms the flukes of the anchor. Knowing how to find the Anchor Tree is somewhat of a local knowledge test - a bit like knowing how to find Music Camp was forty years ago.

January 7, 2006 - Sarah's Birthday

Cake supplied by Eric, Scott, and Ryan. Birthday by Sarah.

On an obscure trail in a spot overlooking the ocean there is a plaque dated Christmas Eve, 1969. It proclaims it's author's sincere love for his girlfriend. This was one of our destinations on this run. There are other plaques around. There's the aviator's plaque near East Peak, and another one from 1992 dedicating Ted Cooper bridge, and one proclaiming a small footbridge to be an Eagle Scout project. Supposedly the Hogan Tree, near Hidden Lake, had a plaque at one time. Now, no one seems to know which tree is or was the Hogan Tree. Speculation is that it fell years ago, plaque side down. There was also a plaque on a stone pedestal at the end of a short trail near Van Wyck Meadow commemorating those who died in World War I. Several years ago someone apparently thought it would look better on their wall, (or possibly on their ebay auction), and it went missing; only the pedestal remains.
